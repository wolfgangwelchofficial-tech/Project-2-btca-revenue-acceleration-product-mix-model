# SKU Velocity & Margin Modeling

## Objective

Identify which SKUs drive the most meaningful revenue and margin impact in order to guide product mix optimization decisions.

---

## 1. SKU Velocity

**Definition:**  
Average units sold per day for a given SKU.

**Formula:**

Total Units Sold / Number of Days Observed

Example (SKU001 – Cocktails):

Total Units = 45 + 50 + 42 + 55 = 192  
Days Observed = 4  

Velocity = 192 / 4 = 48 units per day

---

## 2. Revenue Contribution per SKU

**Formula:**

SUM(net_revenue) per SKU

This identifies top revenue drivers independent of volume.

Champagne (SKU002) shows lower velocity than cocktails but higher revenue contribution due to price positioning.

---

## 3. Gross Margin per SKU

**Formula:**

(unit_price - unit_cost)

Example:

Cocktails (SKU001):  
18.00 - 4.50 = 13.50 margin per unit

Champagne (SKU002):  
160.00 - 90.00 = 70.00 margin per unit

Service Fee (SKU005):  
100.00 - 0.00 = 100.00 margin per unit

---

## 4. Margin-Weighted Velocity

To prioritize optimization opportunities, evaluate:

Velocity × Gross Margin

This identifies SKUs that combine:
- Strong demand
- High profitability

---

## Strategic Insight Example

Even if Champagne has lower velocity than Cocktails, its margin-weighted impact may exceed volume leaders.

This supports strategic actions such as:
- Increasing exposure of high-margin items
- Incentivizing staff to upsell margin-heavy SKUs
- Adjusting inventory mix toward profitability

---

## Key Takeaway

Revenue growth acceleration does not require more traffic — it requires:

- Identifying high-impact SKUs
- Shifting exposure toward margin-weighted velocity leaders
- Reducing capital tied up in low-velocity inventory
